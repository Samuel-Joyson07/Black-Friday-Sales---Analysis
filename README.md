The Black Friday Sales Data project analyzes customer purchasing behaviour during the Black Friday shopping event, which is known for major discounts and high retail activity.
The dataset contains over 537,000 transactions and 12 columns, including user demographics, product categories and purchase amounts.
The analysis begins by loading the dataset into pandas, inspecting its structure and checking for missing or inconsistent data using functions such as df.info().
Individual columns such as Gender, Age, Marital Status and Product Categories are explored to understand their distributions and unique values.
Sparse columns like Product_Category_2 and Product_Category_3 are removed to simplify the dataset and focus on more reliable variables.
Gender analysis reveals a larger proportion of male customers, who also contribute to a higher share of purchases.
Age groups and marital status are examined to identify which demographic segments spend more during the sale.
Multi-column analysis combines Age, Gender and Marital Status to uncover deeper patterns in purchasing behaviour.
Further analysis explores how occupation influences product preferences and spending patterns across categories.
Overall, the project uses pandas, groupby operations and visualizations to uncover insights into customer demographics and product demand during Black Friday sales.
